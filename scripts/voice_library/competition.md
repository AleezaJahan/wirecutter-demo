# "The Competition" / "Other Products We Considered" Examples

These sections explain why rejected products didn't make the cut. Wirecutter is
specific about what failed. Never vague dismissals.

---

## Example 1: Robot Vacuums

We tested the Dyson 360 Vis Nav and were less than impressed. It didn't clean any better than our former budget pick, the Eufy RoboVac 11S Max, despite costing roughly four times as much. While it boasts several nifty features, such as a carrying handle and a "side duct" that redirects suction to capture dust and debris close to baseboards and furniture, it has too short a run time and suffers from too many glitches for a robot of its price.

The Eureka E10S is almost ridiculously bad. Our review unit talked incessantly and cleaned shamefully, and we encountered a different type of problem with each run. At first it spoke only Russian, even though we had the country set to the United States. Then, after mapping the home, it failed to find its base. Lastly, it obsessed over an obstacle-free 4-square-foot area for 15 minutes straight.

## Example 3: Coffee Makers

The sleekly designed Balmuda The Brew made a very good cup of coffee. However, it brews only 12 ounces in total, just barely two cups, which is too little to justify this model's hefty $700 price. The machine also did not keep the coffee warm.

With a multitude of brew settings, the Breville Precision Brewer Thermal has a lot to offer to craft-coffee lovers. But we found it tough to get going — it took us more than two hours to dial in a decent brew. For those who love to tinker or are particular about coffee, the time investment may be rewarding. But if you're just looking to make a good pot of coffee, the learning curve is needlessly steep.

## Voice notes

- **Name the specific failure.** "Spoke only Russian", "brews only 12 ounces", "took more than two hours to dial in". Never "didn't meet our standards".
- **Humor is allowed when earned.** "Almost ridiculously bad", "obsessed over an obstacle-free 4-square-foot area for 15 minutes straight". But it comes from observed absurdity, not from trying to be funny.
- **Acknowledge what the product does well before explaining why it fell short.** "Made a very good cup of coffee. However..."
- **Price-to-value framing.** "Despite costing roughly four times as much", "too little to justify this model's hefty $700 price".
