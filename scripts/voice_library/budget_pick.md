# Budget Pick Write-Up Examples

Budget picks follow the same structure as top picks but position the product
relative to the top pick. They're honest about what you give up at a lower price.

---

## Example 1: Tapo RV30 Max Plus (Robot Vacuum — Runner-up/Budget)

**Headline:** Solid performer, less refined

**Summary blurb:** This affordable robot vacuum has strong cleaning power, and it comes with a charging dock that empties its dustbin after cleaning. But its performance isn't quite as polished as that of our top pick.

**Write-up:**

The Tapo RV30 Max Plus is a simple yet effective machine. Though its performance isn't quite as polished as that of our top pick, this model offers strong cleaning at a reasonable price (it's often on sale), and it comes with a self-emptying dock.

It's a quick, effective, and nimble cleaner. Equipped with lidar-assisted mapping and navigation, the RV30 Max Plus is able to quickly map spaces and clean them thoroughly, hoovering up debris with aplomb. It was among the best in the field at picking up deep-set dust within carpets (within an ounce of measured dust pickup from carpets compared with our top pick), and it especially impressed when cleaning around chair legs and at the edges of our test carpet. But it couldn't quite match our top pick's thoroughness across the board.

When vacuuming a concentrated spill of Cheerios and birdseed, the Tapo wasn't able to pick up as much debris in a single pass as our more powerful top pick could.

**Flaws but not dealbreakers:**

It misses some spots. Corners and edges challenge all robot vacuums, including the RV30 Max Plus and its one side brush. In our tests, it missed some Cheerios and birdseed under a bathroom sink and a kitchen table (most of the other robot vacuums we tested missed debris in these areas too).

This model lagged slightly behind our top pick in overall pickup performance, likely because it can't match the suction of the Roborock Q7 M5+, which is about twice as powerful.

## Example 2: Blueair Blue Pure 511 (Air Purifier — Budget Pick)

**Headline:** Tiny standout for small rooms

**Summary blurb:** Not much bigger than a 3-liter soda bottle, this quiet, energy-efficient purifier is a good choice for dorm rooms, offices, or children's bedrooms.

**Write-up:**

For small spaces, like bedrooms, dorm rooms, and offices, the Blueair Blue Pure 511 is an inexpensive, easy-to-live-with, and effective purifier. Its small size and light weight mean it can sit on a desk or shelf (as well as on the floor), and its understated looks and automatic display shutoff keep it from being a visual distraction.

In our tests, the 511 reduced smoke levels by 96.9% in 30 minutes on its highest speed, and by 89.6% on its medium speed. Even on its high setting it emitted just 48 decibels of fan-like white noise — quiet enough to sleep, work, or watch TV near.

It's extremely energy efficient — drawing just 1.2, 3.5, and 13.6 watts on its low, medium, and high fan settings. The filter lasts about 6 months; a replacement costs about $20.

**Flaws but not dealbreakers:**

Less a flaw than something to be aware of: Because the 511 is so lightweight (less than 3 pounds), it's quite easy to knock over.

## Example 3: Soundcore Space One (Headphones — Budget Pick)

**Headline:** The best budget noise-cancelling headphones

**Write-up:**

If you want a surprisingly great pair of wireless noise-cancelling headphones for around $100, we recommend the Soundcore Space One. In our tests, the active noise cancellation was very effective, and the level is adjustable. The sound, while not as refined as that of the Sony WH-1000XM6, is nonetheless enjoyable to listen to and can be customized to your preference in the Soundcore app. The chassis is lightweight and comfortable to wear, with full controls.

The 40-hour battery life with ANC on (55 hours with it off) means you may only have to charge this pair once a week or less.

While this set has three noise-reducing microphones, we found that voices can sound compressed and somewhat distant to callers — especially when compared with more expensive headphones.

## Voice notes

- **Position against the top pick explicitly.** "Not quite as polished as our top pick", "not as refined as the Sony WH-1000XM6". The reader should understand what they're trading off.
- **Lead with what you get at this price**, not what you lose.
- **Specific numbers justify the value.** "$100", "40-hour battery life", "$20 replacement filter".
- **Same structure: bold claim, evidence, flaws.** Budget picks aren't treated as lesser. They get the same rigor.
