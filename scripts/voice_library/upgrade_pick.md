# Upgrade Pick Write-Up Examples

Upgrade picks justify the higher price with specific capabilities the top pick
doesn't have. They're honest about whether the premium is worth it for most people.

---

## Example 1: Blueair Blue Pure 211i Max (Air Purifier — Upgrade Pick)

**Headline:** A stellar performer for larger rooms

**Summary blurb:** The Blue Pure 211i Max aced our tests, and on top of its powerful performance, it stands out for its energy efficiency, quiet operation, user-friendly features, and good looks.

**Write-up:**

If you need to purify the air in a space of 500 square feet or so, we recommend the Blueair Blue Pure 211i Max. It's extremely powerful and capable of delivering our recommended four ACH (air changes per hour) in spaces as large as 750 square feet on its highest setting — though for noise reasons, you probably wouldn't want to run it that way all the time. Large-room purifiers are physically bigger than their standard-space kin; the 211i Max is 21.5 inches tall and 14 inches wide.

It's also an exceptional performer, dropping the smoke levels in our test space by more than 99.9% on its high and medium-high settings. And it's easy to live with: quiet, attractive, and energy efficient, with a full display shutoff as well as both manual and smart controls. The relatively high price is backed up by the performance, but this much bigger purifier is overkill for medium-size and small rooms.

It's remarkably quiet, particularly for a machine of its capability. On the high setting, it barely broke our 50-decibel limit for comfortable conversation, sleep, or TV watching.

We like the look of it. The tweed-like cover (which also acts as a prefilter) is washable and, when the time comes, replaceable.

**Flaws but not dealbreakers:**

The fabric cover takes on a shaggy look over time as hair and dust bunnies accumulate on its surface, and in our long-term testing it lost its elasticity after a few wash cycles, necessitating replacement.

## Example 2: Technivorm Moccamaster KBT (Coffee Maker — Upgrade/Co-pick)

**Headline:** Great coffee, no programming

**Summary blurb:** This reliable workhorse turns out a nuanced, flavorful pot and comes with a much longer warranty than our other picks.

**Write-up:**

Technivorm brewers have legions of devoted fans because of their simplicity, longevity, and ability to make a great cup of coffee. Their tall, streamlined design features a bare-bones interface, and they come with a five-year warranty, which is better than OXO's two-year one. And all of our tasters enjoyed their coffee, rating them highest in every round.

They make a terrific cup of coffee. The Moccamaster KBT and KB produced well-rounded, nuanced, high-quality cups. The KBT made and kept the coffee very hot.

They brew quickly. Both models turned out a full pot of coffee in just seven minutes.

They're pricier than our other top pick, but they make excellent coffee and have a long, five-year warranty and a history of durability.

The warranty is impressive. With a five-year guarantee behind them, the Moccamaster models have the longest warranty of any machine we tested. And anecdotally, Technivorm brewers have a reputation for lasting a long time.

**Flaws but not dealbreakers:**

The bare-bones design has some drawbacks. The Moccamaster machines don't have a clock, a descaling reminder, or an indication — either a beep or a light — of when the machine is finished brewing.

They are pricier than our top pick from OXO. Refurbished models are available at a discount on the manufacturer's website, however.

## Voice notes

- **Justify the premium with specifics.** "500 square feet", "five-year warranty", "99.9% smoke reduction". Not "better performance".
- **Say who needs this and who doesn't.** "Overkill for medium-size and small rooms." Wirecutter saves readers money when the upgrade isn't warranted.
- **Same structure as top pick.** Bold claim, evidence, flaws. The upgrade pick isn't treated as an afterthought.
