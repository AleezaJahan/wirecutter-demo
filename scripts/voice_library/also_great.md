# "Also Great" / Specific Use Case Pick Examples

These picks serve a niche. The write-up opens by naming the specific use case,
then explains why this product serves it better than the top pick.

---

## Example 1: Eufy L60 (Robot Vacuum — Also Great for Pet Hair)

If you want a robot vacuum that is equipped to handle hair and fur particularly well: The Eufy L60 performed on a par with our picks in our cleaning tests, but it really shined when it came to managing hair and fur. Its self-emptying dock comes with a smartly designed (and effective) detangler built to keep its brush roll free of hair and fur so you don't need to cut strands out yourself.

---

## Example 2: Oransi Airmend Small Room (Air Purifier — Also Great for Rough Spaces)

The Oransi Airmend Small Room has a clever design resembling a compact box fan. At just 13 by 13 by 8 inches and 6.5 pounds, it's easy to pick up and move around, and it can sit comfortably on a desk, workbench, or table without crowding the space. It's also built for durability, with a high-strength housing and a notably thick HEPA filter rated to last up to 18 months between replacements. For these reasons, we think the Airmend Small Room is a great fit for a dorm room or a home workshop.

It was an excellent performer in our testing, dropping smoke levels by 99% and 96% on its highest and midlevel fan speeds, respectively.

---

## Example 3: Fellow Aiden (Coffee Maker — Also Great for Tinkerers)

If you enjoy tinkering with brew settings: The Fellow Aiden Precision Coffeemaker has a modern, high-tech design, streamlined controls, and the ability to brew ahead. The machine offers advanced settings for temperature, coffee-to-water ratio, and bloom and pulse variations so you can dial in the brew that is best for you.

But it took an agonizing 10 minutes 15 seconds to brew a full pot, the carafe seemed to lose heat more quickly than many of the other brands we tested, and a full carafe is prone to spilling when poured.

---

## Voice notes

- **Open with the use case as a conditional.** "If you want...", "If you enjoy...", "For those who need...". The reader self-selects.
- **Explain what makes this pick better for that use case than the top pick.** Not just "also good". Specifically better at something.
- **Still honest about trade-offs.** "An agonizing 10 minutes", "this much bigger purifier is overkill".
